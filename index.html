Current Status — 2025 Update

Understanding the current situation and the opportunity to honor Frank Merz's original intent.

🌟 Opportunity for Community Use

Resident summer camp at Camp Merz did not operate in 2025. The camp's official page lists all 2025 sessions as "All sessions cancelled." This creates an opportunity to ensure the land continues to serve local youth as Frank Merz intended. Source.

📰 Local Reporting

AHC told families it had "made the difficult decision not to operate our summer camp in 2025." The Post‑Journal/Observer Today summarized the letter and reported Camp Merz closed its operations for the summer (resident camp). The article also says the council planned to use the property for short‑term rental (Airbnb) during the shuttering. Source.

🏕️ Short‑term Use Continues

Short‑term use continues: The council's reservation portal and facility pages still show the property available for short‑term/unit reservations during the year. Source, Source.

🌊 Broader Regional Trends

The same coverage attributes closures at multiple area camps to cost‑saving measures and post‑COVID headwinds: lower enrollment, extended school calendars, lingering health concerns, screen‑time habits, weather, and staffing/international camper declines. These regional trends create opportunities for communities to reimagine how these valuable properties can serve local families.

Our Vision for Camp Merz Park

We believe in honoring Frank Merz's original intent. Our mission is to transform this sacred land into a public park that serves ALL families and youth of Chautauqua County—just as Frank intended.

🏞️ Preserve Public Access

Protect the pristine shoreline and 100+ acres of forest for educational programs, camping, paddling, and nature exploration. This land belongs to the community.

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Serve All Youth

Keep faith with Frank Merz's original intent: a place dedicated to the growth, learning, and well-being of local youth. His legacy must live on.

The Man Behind the Legacy

Frank Merz's story is the story of Chautauqua County itself—a tale of humble beginnings, hard work, and deep commitment to community.

Frank Merz (1857–1939) rose from humble beginnings to become one of Jamestown's most respected bankers and community leaders. He founded the Union Trust Company in 1893 and served as chairman of the board, but his greatest legacy was his belief in opportunity for young people. When he donated the land for Camp Merz, he envisioned a place where local youth could learn leadership, build character, and develop a love for the outdoors that would last a lifetime.

"The land was a gift for the youth of this community. Let's make sure it stays that way."

Camp Merz: A Place of Memories

For generations, this lakeside haven has been where friendships were forged, skills were learned, and the love of Chautauqua Lake was passed from one generation to the next.

Situated just outside Mayville, NY—at the heart of Chautauqua County—Camp Merz has deep ties to Jamestown, Chautauqua Lake, and the broader region. Countless alumni remember summers filled with camping, canoeing, campfires, and community. The camp's closure represents not just the loss of a facility, but the potential loss of a sacred space that has shaped thousands of lives.

James "Jim" Butterfield (in the blue Camp Merz ballcap), grandson of Frank Merz, was a tireless advocate for Camp Merz. He is pictured above at Camp Merz with Frank Merz granddaughter Margaret "Peg" Butterfield Seiniger and a number of Frank Merz's great-grandchildren and great-great grandchildren.

History & Timeline of Camp Merz

Nearly a century of service to Chautauqua County's youth—from Frank Merz's original gift to today's urgent need to preserve this legacy.

📅 90+ Years of History at Risk

Camp Merz sits just south of Mayville on Chautauqua Lake. The property was acquired in the early 1930s through the generosity of Jamestown banker Frank Merz and has hosted generations of local youth for waterfront and outdoor programs. Source, Source.

  • 1932

    County historian records note: "Camp Merz was donated to the Boy Scouts." Source.

  • 1933

    Property purchased for the Boy Scouts through a donation by Frank Merz. Source.

  • 1935

    Camp Merz opens for scouts (first mid‑1930s seasons reported). Source.

  • 1950s–2010s

    Camp builds a regional reputation for aquatics and outdoor skills on a large lakefront site. Current descriptions cite ~350 acres and 1,500 feet of private waterfront. Source.

  • 2020

    The Allegheny Highlands Council (AHC) suspended in‑person summer camps due to the COVID‑19 pandemic.

  • 2021

    To address financial pressure from the national BSA bankruptcy, AHC voted to sell Elk Lick Scout Reserve in PA (Camp Merz remained).

  • 2023

    Community marks ~90 years of Camp Merz; local coverage highlights the Merz gift and ongoing programs. Source.

  • 2025

    Resident summer sessions at Camp Merz are canceled (Weeks of July 6, 13, 20 listed as "All sessions cancelled"). Source.

About the donor: Frank Merz (1857–1939) led the Union Trust Company in Jamestown and helped secure the lakeside property for youth camping. Source.

Frank Merz: A Life of Service

From humble beginnings to community leadership, Frank Merz's story embodies the American dream and the spirit of Chautauqua County.

Frank Merz's legacy extends far beyond his banking career. His most lasting gift—Camp Merz on Chautauqua Lake—represents his deep belief in opportunity for young people and his commitment to the enduring spirit of civic generosity. We owe it to his memory to ensure this land continues to serve the youth of Chautauqua County.

Frank Merz's Philanthropic and Cultural Legacy

Chautauqua Lake and Camp Merz have been part of the extended Merz family story for generations. Frank educated his daughters to have a love for the beauty of Chautauqua County and Chautauqua Lake. Two of his daughters, Elizabeth and Margaret were distinguished musicians. All of Frank Merz many descendants blessed by his passion for the culture of Chautauqua County have been devoted to the arts and Chautauqua Lake as well as Camp Merz.

Elizabeth Merz Butterfield

Distinguished Musician

Frank's daughter, Elizabeth Butterfield Merz, was a prominent composer with ties to Chautauqua Institution. She won a national patriotic song competition and performed it for Eleanor Roosevelt in the White House, further connecting the Merz family to the cultural heritage of Chautauqua County.

Margaret "Peggy" Butterfield Seiniger

Frank Merz granddaughter who was a long time Jamestown/Chautauqua Institute resident who sang in the Chautauqua Opera

Margaret Merz Richardson

Distinguished Musician

For years Margaret Merz Richardson played violin in The Amity String Quartet with three senior members of The Washington National Symphony. She carried on the philanthropic legacy of Frank Merz, volunteering through St. Columba's Church to help neglected residents at Washington DC's Home For the Incurables. She started Sargeant House at St. Columba's church, an outreach program for seniors.

Wm. Breck Seiniger, Jr. (a.k.a. "Hooley The Poet")

Great-grandson of Frank Merz

"After my mother (Peg Seiniger) passed, we honored her wishes by returning her to Chautauqua County near the lake she so adored. The song 'Heaven's Lake' celebrates our family's memories of seeing the lake gleam in the sunshine from the top of the hill in Mayville, not far from the land that Frank Merz donated for the benefit of local youth. These treasured memories underscore why the land my great-grandfather donated for the benefit of local youth deserves to remain for the benefit of Chautauqua County community's youth."
